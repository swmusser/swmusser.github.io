Summary

Check out my CV.

My goal as a physicist is to probe the role that topology plays in fundamental physics phenomena from the quantum Hall effect (QHE) to vortex dynamics in a superfluid. My broad interest in topology has often led me to interesting numerical work. For example, my interest in the topological aspects of superfluid vortex motion, as described by Scheeler et al., led to my work with the Irvine lab at UChicago. While there I built from scratch a GPU-enhanced simulation of a superfluid to study vortex nucleation from hydrofoils. At Cambridge my interest in the QHE led to an interest in particle-vortex duality. I wrote my Part III essay on particle-vortex duality, using my earlier code to display superfluid vortices, and writing additional code to reproduce Halperin and Dasgupta’s numerical demonstration of particle-vortex duality. You can find more information about these efforts below.

I am also passionate about helping students to think about physics pictorially and to appreciate and take advantage of symmetry in their work. I plan to write a blog post soon describing my philosophy of physics instruction, and why David Tong’s notes are (in my opinion) so effective at teaching physics.

Tutoring

I offer my tutoring services to both high school and college students and will gladly tutor a student on any subtopic of mathematics or physics. I worked for four years as a calculus tutor at UChicago so I have particular expertise there. Additionally, I am more than willing to offer test-taking advice for the: AP tests, SAT, SAT Math 2, SAT Physics, GRE, and GRE Physics. Finally, I can offer guidance on applying for nationally competitive fellowships and scholarships, having myself received the: Barry Goldwater Scholarship, Churchill Scholarship, and NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

My pricing is as follows:
